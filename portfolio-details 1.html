London Crime

  • The London crime dataset is a collection of data that provides information about reported crimes in the city of London. Analyzing this dataset can provide valuable insights into crime patterns, trends, and other relevant information.
  • Project URL: London Crime

Key insights

  • ﻿After analyzing the trend line in the Power BI line chart, we have observed a consistent downward trend. This indicates that there has been a reduction in crime from 2008 to 2016.
  • ﻿London experiences significant criminal activity, with theft and handling being the most common offense at 41.28% of all reported crimes. Other frequently reported crimes include violence, burglary, drug-related offenses, fraud/forgery, and sexual offenses.
  • ﻿Upon analysis of the data, it has been revealed that the area with the highest total value of crime is South London, with a staggering number of 2203924. Following closely behind in descending order are West London, East London, and North London.
  • ﻿London has experienced a notable amount of criminal activity, ranging from minor offenses to serious ones. The top 10 minor crimes include theft, theft from motor vehicles, burglary in dwellings, harassment, assault causing injury, possession of drugs, common assault, theft from shops, theft from individuals, and criminal damage to motor vehicles. Of these, Other Theft accounts for 20.78% of the total crime rate.
  • ﻿Westminster stands out as the most dangerous place to live with a significantly high count of 455,028 reported crimes, far surpassing any other locality. In contrast, the City of London has the lowest total crime rate, with only 780 reported crimes
